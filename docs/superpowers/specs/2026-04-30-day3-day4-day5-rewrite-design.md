### Overview

First full day in Florence — no bookings, no timed entries. The goal is orientation: get a feel for the city's geography, walk the key public spaces, cross the river into Oltrarno, and end the day with sunset from Piazzale Michelangelo. Note: June 7 is Sunday — Mercato Centrale ground-floor market vendors are mostly closed, but the upstairs food hall is open.

### Morning: City Center (9 AM – 12 PM)

**Breakfast** — Find a neighborhood bar near the hotel. Order at the counter: *"Un cappuccino e un cornetto, per favore."* Standing is cheaper and more local. Cost: €3–5.

**Piazza della Signoria**

The political heart of Florence and one of the great outdoor spaces in Italy. Write a table of what to see:

| What | Why It Matters |
|----|----|
| **Loggia dei Lanzi** | Open-air sculpture gallery — Cellini's *Perseus with Medusa's Head*, Giambologna's *Rape of the Sabines*. Free, no entry required. Extraordinary. |
| **Copy of Michelangelo's David** | Marks the spot where the original stood for 300+ years before moving to the Accademia. |
| **Fontana del Nettuno** | Ammannati's massive Neptune fountain — Florentines call it "il Biancone" (the big white one). |
| **Palazzo Vecchio** | Florence's medieval town hall, still in use. The tower is the city's most recognizable silhouette. |

Allow 45–60 minutes. Sit on the steps, watch the city, take it in.

**Walk to Piazza del Duomo**

10-minute walk north. See the Duomo exterior — the facade, the green-and-white marble, the scale. This is admiring from outside; the interior and Ghiberti Pass are tomorrow (Day 4). The Campanile (Giotto's Bell Tower) alongside it is worth noting as a landmark — one of the finest Gothic towers in Italy. You'll go inside the complex properly tomorrow.

### Afternoon: Ponte Vecchio & Oltrarno (1 – 5 PM)

**Lunch** — Options near Piazza della Signoria or on the way to the bridge: - **All'Antico Vinaio** (Via dei Neri 74) — famous schiacciata sandwiches, expect a queue. Worth it. - Any trattoria on the side streets south of the Uffizi.

**Ponte Vecchio**

Medieval bridge (1345) lined with jewelry shops. Walk across — browse if interested, but know prices are high. The bridge itself is the point: views up and down the Arno, the enclosed corridor above (Vasari Corridor, not currently open), the sense of continuity with medieval Florence.

**Oltrarno**

Cross Ponte Vecchio and spend the afternoon in the neighborhood on the other side of the river. Less touristy, more craft-workshop character.

| Street / Spot | What to Find |
|----|----|
| **Via Maggio** | Antique dealers, high-end shops, palazzo facades |
| **Piazza Santo Spirito** | Local piazza with a market on Sunday mornings; Brunelleschi's church |
| **Borgo San Jacopo** | Quiet streets parallel to the river |
| **Leather and paper goods** | Florence is still a center for artisan bookbinding and leather — look for workshops rather than tourist shops |

No agenda here — wander, look in windows, sit at a café.

### Evening: Piazzale Michelangelo & Dinner (6 – 10 PM)

**Piazzale Michelangelo**

Panoramic overlook above the south bank of the Arno. Best view of Florence: Duomo, Palazzo Vecchio, the river, the hills. Sunset is around 8:30 PM in June — arrive by 8:00.

Getting there from Oltrarno: Bus #12 from Piazza Ferrucci is the easiest option (drops you right at the piazzale). If walking, go via Viale Galileo Galilei (road, no stairs) — about 25 minutes.

At the top: bronze copy of David, gelato vendors, tourists, and one of the genuinely great views in Italy. Stay through golden hour.

**Dinner**

Walk or bus back down into Oltrarno. This neighborhood has some of Florence's best restaurants at more reasonable prices than the tourist center.

What to try on Day 3: - *Pappardelle al cinghiale* — wide pasta with wild boar ragù, the quintessential Tuscan dish - *Ribollita* — hearty Tuscan bean and bread soup - House Chianti by the carafe

## Day 4 — June 8 (Monday): Duomo Content Additions

Day 4 keeps its existing Ghiberti Pass confirmed content unchanged. Add the following sections from Day 3, adapted:

### Add: Cathedral Interior (from floor level)

After the "Also Valid on Your Ghiberti Pass" section, add a subsection on the Cathedral nave:

**Cathedral Nave (Interior)**

Entry to the nave is free and included with the Ghiberti Pass. The scale is the first thing that hits you — the Duomo is the third-largest church in the world.

| What to See | Notes |
|----|----|
| **Vasari's *Last Judgment* fresco** | Covers the entire interior of the dome — visible from the floor without climbing |
| **Scale and emptiness** | Deliberately austere interior; the exterior decoration is the show |
| **Paolo Uccello's clock** | Unusual liturgical clock on the west wall, runs counter-clockwise |
| **Equestrian frescoes** | Trompe l'oeil paintings of condottieri (mercenary commanders) — John Hawkwood and Niccolò da Tolentino |

Time: 20–30 minutes.

### Add: The Campanile (Giotto's Bell Tower) — Exterior Note

The Campanile stands immediately next to the Duomo — 84 meters of Florentine Gothic. Worth a close look at the marble relief panels on the lower registers (originals now in the Opera del Duomo Museum you're visiting). No climb; the exterior and the panels are the point.
