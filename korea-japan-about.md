## The Trip

We're embarking on an incredible journey through two of East Asia's most fascinating countries—South Korea and Japan. This site documents our planning, shares our itinerary, and will serve as a travel journal as we explore ancient temples, modern cities, incredible food, and rich cultural traditions.

The journey is made even more special by meaningful connections: in Korea, we'll be reuniting with good friends who call Seoul home, getting an insider's perspective on Korean life and culture. Then we'll join the Wisconsin Quilt Museum's tour group for an art-focused exploration of Japan, diving deep into textile traditions and contemporary creativity.

## Why Korea & Japan?

These two neighboring countries offer a perfect blend of ancient history and cutting-edge modernity. From Seoul's vibrant neighborhoods and palaces to Kyoto's serene temples, from Korean BBQ to fresh sushi, from traditional tea ceremonies to neon-lit streets—we're ready to experience it all.

The trip combines two distinct experiences: we'll start in Korea, where we have dear friends who will show us their country through local eyes, followed by a guided cultural and historical tour. Then we'll continue to Japan as part of the Wisconsin Quilt Museum's art and culture tour led by Heidi Parkes, exploring Japanese textile traditions, contemporary art, and cultural sites.

## Our Approach

We're fortunate to experience these countries through multiple lenses: the local knowledge of friends in Korea, structured cultural tours, and the Wisconsin Quilt Museum's carefully curated art itinerary in Japan. We're blending guided experiences with free time to wander, mixing famous landmarks with local hangouts, and leaving room for spontaneity. The goal is to immerse ourselves in the culture, try as much local food as possible, and return with stories worth telling.

This site is equal parts planning tool and travel journal—a living document that will grow and evolve as we prepare for and embark on this adventure.
